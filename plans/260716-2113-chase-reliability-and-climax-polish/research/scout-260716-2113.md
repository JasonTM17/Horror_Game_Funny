# Chase Reliability and Climax Scout

## Runtime/Specification Findings

- Capture recovery awaits 1.25 seconds, then restores checkpoint and chase without rechecking whether Ending became terminal. Programmatic overlap can replace `ENDING` with `CHASE` and overwrite the ending objective.
- Chase dressing uses non-colliding visual meshes and one rectangular navigation polygon. The required blocked-route climax is therefore a straight 300-unit sprint.
- Fuse visual state, locked-door rejection cooldown, fall recovery, restart routing, and synchronous inventory transactions remain valid later follow-ups; they are not silently folded into this plan.

## Pacing Findings

- Critical path: about 596.5 seconds at sprint and 741 seconds mostly walking.
- Chapter estimates at sprint: opening 68.8 seconds, Floor 4 84.3, memory 250.9, Room 407 92.8, chase/ending 99.8.
- Current 70 OGG cues total 233.994 seconds raw and about 299.015 seconds effective authored hold, but narration usually overlaps movement.
- A two-step six-line interactive epilogue adds roughly 24 meaningful seconds and brings the mathematical chase/ending minimum inside its 120–180 second target without a new level or idle timer.

## Decision

Fix the terminal race first, then add navigation-safe chase decisions, then replace the passive ending delay with player-controlled voiced investigation. This sequence protects correctness before changing topology or pacing.
